# Article 71: EU database for high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III

## Chapter VIII: EU Database for High-Risk AI Systems

### Summary

This article describes the establishment and maintenance of an EU database for high-risk AI systems and other AI systems that are subject to registration requirements under AI Act. Most of the information will be publicly accessible, except for certain sections related to real-world testing and specific AI systems. The database will include minimal personal data necessary for compliance and will be controlled by the European Commission, which will provide technical support and ensure accessibility.

The summaries are meant to provide helpful explanation but are not legal binding.

### Article 71: EU database for high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III

#### Summary

This article describes the establishment and maintenance of an EU database containing information referred to in paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Article concerning high-risk AI systems referred to in [Article 6(2)](/en/ai-act/article-6) which are registered in accordance with Articles 49 and 60 and AI systems that are not considered as high-risk pursuant to [Article 6(3)](/en/ai-act/article-6) and which are registered in accordance with [Article 6(4)](/en/ai-act/article-6) and [Article 49](/en/ai-act/article-49). When setting the functional specifications of such database, the Commission shall consult the relevant experts, and when updating the functional specifications of such database, the Commission shall consult the Board.

#### Data Listing

**Sections A & B**

The data listed in Sections A & B of [Annex VIII](/en/ai-act/annex-8) shall be entered into the EU database by:

* Provider or, where applicable, by the authorised representative.

**Section C**

The data listed in Section C of [Annex VIII](/en/ai-act/annex-8) shall be entered into the EU database by:

* Deployer who is, or who acts on behalf of, a public authority, agency or body, in accordance with [Article 49(3)](/en/ai-act/article-49) and (4).

#### Personal Data Access

With the exception of the section referred to in [Article 49(4)](/en/ai-act/article-49) and [Article 60(4)](/en/ai-act/article-60), point (c), the information contained in the EU database registered in accordance with [Article 49](/en/ai-act/article-49) shall be accessible and publicly available in a user-friendly manner. The information should be easily navigable and machine-readable. The information registered in accordance with [Article 60](/en/ai-act/article-60) shall be accessible only to market surveillance authorities and the Commission, unless the prospective provider or provider has given consent for also making the information accessible to the public.

#### Privacy Protection

The EU database shall contain personal data only in so far as necessary for collecting and processing information in accordance with this Regulation. That information shall include:
* Names and contact details of natural persons who are responsible for registering the system.
* Legal authority to represent either:
	+ Provider
	+ Deployer
