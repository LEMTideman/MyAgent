# Art. 66 AI Act - Tasks of the Board

**1** The Board shall advise and assist the Commission and the Member States in order to facilitate the consistent and effective application of this Regulation. **2** To that end, the Board may in particular:

1. contribute to the coordination among national competent authorities responsible for the application of this Regulation and, in cooperation with and subject to the agreement of the market surveillance authorities concerned, support joint activities of market surveillance authorities referred to in [Article 74(11)](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/74);
2. collect and share technical and regulatory expertise and best practices among Member States;
3. provide advice on the implementation of this Regulation, in particular as regards the enforcement of rules on general-purpose AI models;
4. contribute to the harmonization of administrative practices in the Member States, including in relation to the derogation from the conformity assessment procedures referred to in [Article 46](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/46), the functioning of AI regulatory sandboxes, and testing in real-world conditions referred to in [Articles 57](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/57), [59](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/59) and [60](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/60);
5. at the request of the Commission or on its own initiative, issue recommendations and written opinions on any relevant matters related to the implementation of this Regulation and to its consistent and effective application, including:
   1. on the development and application of codes of conduct and codes of practice pursuant to this Regulation, as well as of the Commissionâ€™s guidelines;
   2. the evaluation and review of this Regulation pursuant to [Article 112](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/112), including as regards the serious incident reports referred to in [Article 73](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/73), and the functioning of the EU database referred to in [Article 71](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/71), the preparation of the delegated or implementing acts, and as regards possible alignments of this Regulation with the Union harmonization legislation listed in [Annex I](https://ai-act-law.eu/annex/1/) ;
   3. on technical specifications or existing standards regarding the requirements set out in [Articles 40](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/40) and [41](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/41);
   4. trends, such as European global competitiveness in AI, the uptake of AI in the Union, and the development of digital skills;
   5. trends on the evolving typology of AI value chains, in particular on the resulting implications in terms of accountability;
   6. on the potential need for amendment to [Annex III](https://ai-act-law.eu/annex/3) in accordance with [Article 7](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/7/) , and on the potential need for possible revision of [Article 5](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/5/) pursuant to [Article 112](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/112) , taking into account relevant available evidence and the latest developments in technology;
6. support the Commission in promoting AI literacy, public awareness, and understanding of the benefits, risks, safeguards, and rights and obligations related to using AI systems;
7. facilitate the development of common criteria and a shared understanding among market operators and competent authorities of relevant concepts provided for in this Regulation, including by contributing to develop benchmarks;
8. cooperate, as appropriate, with other Union institutions, bodies, offices & agencies, as well as relevant Union expert groups & networks (e.g., product safety; cybersecurity; competition; digital & media services; financial services; consumer protection; data & fundamental rights protection);
9. contribute effectively towards cooperation with competent authorities from third countries & international organizations;
10. assist national competent authorities & Commission in developing organizational & technical expertise required for implementing this Regulation (including by contributing towards assessing training needs for staff employed by Member States involved managing implementations under this Regulation);
11. assist AI Office towards supporting national competent authorities' efforts towards establishing & developing AI regulatory sandboxes (and facilitating information sharing between sandboxes);
12. contribute towards assisting providers & deployers who have already entered onto market or been put into service (of general-purpose AI models) prior to entry into force date (or earlier if applicable); 
    * facilitate communication between these stakeholders about compliance issues related to those models (e.g., via code-of-conduct agreements);
    * liaise with them regarding proposed amendments/additions/revisions made by Commission pursuant to Article 99 etc.;
    * liaise with them regarding upcoming reviews conducted by Commission's Regulatory Impact Assessment Team.
