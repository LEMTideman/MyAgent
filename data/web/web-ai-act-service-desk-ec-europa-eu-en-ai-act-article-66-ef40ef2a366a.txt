# Article 66: Tasks of the Board

## Summary

The Board shall advise and assist the Commission and the Member States in order to facilitate the consistent and effective application of this Regulation. [Article 66](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-66) of the AI Act outlines the responsibilities of the European AI Board. The Board also promotes cooperation, knowledge-sharing, and may issue recommendations and opinions on the development of standards and guidelines.

The summaries are meant to provide helpful explanation but are not legal binding.

## Board's Tasks

The Board shall advise and assist the Commission and the Member States in order to facilitate the consistent and effective application of this Regulation. To that end, the Board may in particular:

- contribute to the coordination among national competent authorities responsible for the application of this Regulation and, in cooperation with and subject to the agreement of the market surveillance authorities concerned, support joint activities of market surveillance authorities referred to in [Article 74(11)](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-74);
- collect and share technical and regulatory expertise and best practices among Member States;
- provide advice on the implementation of this Regulation, in particular as regards the enforcement of rules on general-purpose AI models;
- contribute to the harmonization of administrative practices in the Member States, including in relation to the derogation from the conformity assessment procedures referred to in [Article 46](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-46), the functioning of AI regulatory sandboxes, and testing in real-world conditions referred to in Articles 57, 59, and 60;
- at the request of the Commission or on its own initiative, issue recommendations and written opinions on any relevant matters related to the implementation of this Regulation and to its consistent and effective application:
  - on the development and application of codes of conduct and codes of practice pursuant to this Regulation, as well as of the Commissionâ€™s guidelines;
  - on evaluating and reviewing this Regulation pursuant to [Article 112](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-112), including as regards serious incident reports referred to in [Article 73](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.euola.com/en/ai-act/article-73), and on issues concerning delegated or implementing acts, as well as possible alignments with Union harmonized legislation listed in Annex I;
  - on technical specifications or existing standards regarding requirements set out in Chapter III, Section 2;
  - on using harmonized standards or common specifications referred to in Articles 40 and 41;
  - trends such as European global competitiveness in AI, uptake of AI within Europe, digital skills development,
  - trends on evolving typologies of AI value chains (including accountability),
  - potential need for amendments to Annex III in accordance with Article 7, considering available evidence and recent technological developments;
- support the Commission in promoting AI literacy, public awareness, understanding of benefits, risks, safeguards, rights, and obligations related to AI systems;
- facilitate development of common criteria and shared understanding among market operators and competent authorities about relevant concepts provided for in this Regulation;
- cooperate appropriately with other Union institutions, bodies, offices, agencies, relevant Union expert groups/networks (especially those working in areas like product safety, cybersecurity, competition/digital/media services/services), expert networks (such as those focused on digital skills), financial services (like customer due diligence), consumer protection (for example regarding unfair contract terms), data protection (particularly regarding fundamental rights protection), etc.;
- contribute effectively towards collaboration with third countries/international organizations;
- assist national competent authorities in developing organizational & technical expertise required for implementation according to this Regulation (e.g., assessing training needs for Member State staff involved);
- assist AI Office towards supporting national competent authorities' efforts towards establishment & development of AI regulatory sandboxes & facilitating cooperation & information sharing among AI regulatory sandboxes;
- collaborate with other Union institutions/bodies/offices/agencies where applicable (e.g., DG CONNECT's work with other EU institutions/partnerships), particularly when addressing international matters related to AI technologies;
