# Art. 41 AI Act - Common Specifications

## Main Content

1. The Commission may adopt, implementing acts establishing common specifications for the requirements set out in Section 2 of [this Chapter](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/3/) or, as applicable, for the obligations set out in Sections 2 and 3 of [Chapter V](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/5/) where the following conditions have been fulfilled:

    1. The Commission has requested, pursuant to [Article 10(1)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32012R1025#d1e1208-12-1) of Regulation (EU) No 1025/2012, one or more European standardisation organizations to draft a harmonised standard for the requirements set out in Section 2 of [this Chapter](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/3/), or, as applicable, for the obligations set out in Sections 2 and 3 of [Chapter V](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/5/), and:
    
        1. The request has not been accepted by any of the European standardisation organizations; or
        2. The harmonised standards addressing that request are not delivered within the deadline set in accordance with [Article 10(1)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32012R1025#d1e1208-12-1) of Regulation (EU) No 1025/2012; or
        3. The relevant harmonised standards insufficiently address fundamental rights concerns; or
        4. The harmonised standards do not comply with the request; and
        
    2. No reference to harmonised standards covering the requirements referred to in Section 2 of [this Chapter](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/3/) or, as applicable, the obligations referred to in Sections 2 and 3 of [Chapter V](https://ai-act-law.eu/chapter/5/) has been published in the Official Journal of the European Union in accordance with [Regulation (EU) No 1025/2012](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32012R1025#d1e40-12-1), and no such reference is expected to be published within a reasonable period.

    When drafting the common specifications, the Commission shall consult the advisory forum referred to in [Article 67](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/67/).

    The implementing acts referred to in paragraph 1 shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in [Article 98(2)](https://ai-act-law.eu/article/98/).

    > **Note:** Where a harmonised standard is adopted by a European standardisation organization and proposed to the Commission for publication in the Official Journal of the European Union, the Commission shall assess the harmonised standard in accordance with [Regulation (EU) No 1025/2012](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32012R10). If necessary, amendments can be made to ensure compliance with Article 4.

    > **Note:** When reference to a harmonised standard is published in the Official Journal of the European Union, the Commission shall repeal all implementing acts referring to that standard.

    > **Note:** Where providers of high-risk AI systems or general-purpose AI models do not comply with the common specifications referred to in paragraph 1, they shall duly justify that they have adopted technical solutions meeting these requirements or comply with these obligations at least equivalent thereto.
    
    > **Note:** Where a Member State considers that a common specification does not fully meet the requirements outlined in Section II or fails to comply with obligations under Articles IV-VIII of Chapter V, it should inform the Commission about this issue through an official communication.
